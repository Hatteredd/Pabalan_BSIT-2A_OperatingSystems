Group Policy

Activity Overview

Group Policy is one of the most powerful features of the Windows domain environment. It allows administrators to centrally configure security settings, desktop restrictions, software deployment, scripts, and many other options for users and computers. In this activity you will explore how Group Policy Objects (GPOs) are created, linked, and applied within an Active Directory structure.

You will see the impact of GPOs on different Organizational Units (OUs), observe the order of processing, and test typical policies such as password rules, desktop lockdown, startup scripts, and mapping network drives. The goal is to understand how a single change at the domain controller can affect dozens of machines, which is exactly what makes Group Policy an essential tool in real-world Operating Systems administration.
